Edge effect is a collection of friends and engineering peers; we have previously worked together on various academic and business projects. We have found that our diversity of experiences, interests, and knowledge bases makes for engaging and lively discussion. This group is a way for us to continue these interactions in a productive way even as we move forward separately in our young careers. The hope is to maintain fun social interactions but especially to continue stimulating and educating each other with periodic meetings. The focal point of these meetings is a presentation by a member or members on any intellectual topic of potential utility to the group.

Presentation format

  • Presentation meeting at 6 week intervals, always on Sunday evening, hosted via Google Hangout.
  • Meetings: 1.5 hour expected time: Begin with 30 min (max) presentation and 60 min discussion afterward.
  • Talk: slide deck suggested, other organized and comprehensive medium possible.
  • While the general feel of the group is for engineering design, diversity in topics is encouraged, e.g. advanced academic work, business and industry perspective, contemporary geopolitical/economic assessment.
  • Presentations that connect to tangible ideas, interesting intuition, and applicability in “real” life strongly encouraged.
  • Presenter is expected to provide a 1 page summary of the presentation 1 week prior to the meeting. All audience members are expected to read this document before the meeting.
  • A 1 page summary of the presentation and subsequent discussion will be posted to this web site following each meeting.
  • For more details see the Presentation Instructions, Suggestions, and House Rules section below.

Presentation Instructions, Suggestions, and House Rules

The most important feature of this group is enthusiasm and buy-in. But we must balance this with tradeoffs in time and energy we all face. Consequently, we have chosen a 6-week meeting interval. This interval, along with the number of different presenters, should strike a good balance. A member will be required to present no more than 2 times in one year, and the requirements for audience members is simply to read the 1 page summary ahead of time, block out 1.5 hours for the meeting each 6 weeks, and to show up ready to take part in a lively discussion. Having said that, it is incredibly important that members do take this degree of commitment (very low and diffuse over time) seriously to realize the benefits of this group.
Slide deck suggestions
  • No font smaller than 24 point.
  • No more than 30 slides (e.g. 1 slide per minute).
  • Avoid more than 2 -- 3 big ideas per slide (less is more!).
  • Figures and equations should be clear. Do not force too much on one slide.
Presenter responsibilities
  • Be clear and do not rush through the slides.
  • Responsible for moving forward, in a responsible manner, after interjections (see below).
  • Responsible for writing up 1 page summary of the presentation and discussion after the meeting.
Audience Member Responsibilities
  • Read the 1 page summary provided by the presenter before the meeting.
  • Do not engage in other activities (e.g. reading email, etc.) during the presentation -- respect other members.
  • Interjections during the presentation are allowed if:
    • needed to clarify a point that is important for the talk.
    • long tangents are avoided (discussion points can and should be fleshed out more fully after the end of the presentation).
    • presenter has the right to move on at their discretion.
Group procedures
  • Quality control.
    • Daniel and David will start off as the “editors” for the group. We will read and edit the one page background and one page review of the talks, to be posted on the site. We will check our revisions with the author before posting.
  • Adding new members.
    • Discuss later.
  • Sensitive content.
    • Can optionally provide slides for the website.
